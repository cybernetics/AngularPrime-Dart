This demo shows how versatile the attributes of pui-components are.
With a few exceptions they can
  • be any mixture of literals and angularized expressions ({ { } })
  • be read from the Angulardart model
  • and if they consists of a single AngularDart model variable, the angular braces can be omitted.
    <pui-grid>
        <pui-dropdown ng-model="ctrl.carTable[0].brand" editable="true" options="ctrl.carTable[0].brands" label="brand"></pui-dropdown>
        <pui-dropdown label="type" ng-model="ctrl.carTable[0].type" editable="true" options="ctrl.carTable[0].matchingTypes()"></pui-dropdown>
        <pui-input label="Color of your {{ctrl.carTable[0].brand}} {{ctrl.carTable[0].type}}" ng-model="ctrl.carTable[0].color" required></pui-input>
    </pui-grid>
The cities must comply to the regular expression {{ctrl.cityRegExp}}. 
    <pui-grid>
        <pui-dropdown label="Regular expression protecting the city field" ng-model="ctrl.cityRegExp" editable="true">
